article > .hero > section

Basic implementation of a hero. Style the .hero element, the section element maintains proper spacing.

eg: .hero is blue; hero > section is salmon.

article > .hero > div > section

Alternatively there can be a containing div within the .hero element, which allows for additional full-bleed styling without affecting the entire hero.

eg: .hero > div is dark pink; hero > div > section is light pink

.content is not consider to be full-bleed. At small sizes it will fill the page width, but at larger sizes it is constrained.

At larger scren sizes .content maintains spacing, but smallers sizes child elements are responsible for their own spacing

eg: .content is tan

article > .content > .main > section

The standard implementaion of conten, the section element maintains proper spacing at small screen sizes.

eg: .content > .main > section is green

article > .content > .main > div

Alternatively the section can be replaced with a div which is effectively full-bleed on small screens, but will still be constrained at larger screen sizes.

eg: .content > .main > div is purple

When used in conjunction with .content > .asides, .main will appear first at small screen sizes, but as the second column at larger sizes.

Internal spacing for sections (and divs) when multiple columns are presented are not handled by the default layout

eg: .content > .main > section is blue

The distinction between section and div is still true

eg: .content > .main > div is tan

.asides is presented after .main when shown as a single column, and to the left when shown as multiple columns.

Content in .asides should be wrapped in `aside` elements

eg: .asides > aside is orange

Most aside content can also be wrapped in a section to maintain proper spacing

eg: .asides > aside > section is red

Alt. content can be wrapped in a div to surpress spacing

eg: .asides > aside > div is green
